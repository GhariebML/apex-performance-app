Executive Summary

FINAL REPORT

Body Performance
Final Report

The Snipers Team from the Military Technical College presents the culmination of our AI & Machine Learning final project: a comprehensive body performance intelligence system that classifies individuals into performance tiers (A–D) and predicts broad jump distance with near-perfect accuracy.

13,393 Records Analyzed
5 Classifiers Trained
3 Regression Models
75.1% Best Accuracy (RF)

Our end-to-end pipeline covers data cleaning, exploratory analysis, feature engineering, model training and hyperparameter tuning, evaluation, and deployment — resulting in a production-ready web application backed by a FastAPI inference server.

Key System Insights

FEATURE ANALYSIS
#1
🧘

Flexibility (Sit & Bend)

Importance: 0.261

The single most influential feature in determining performance class. Individuals with higher flexibility scores are disproportionately represented in Class A, confirming that mobility is a cornerstone of overall fitness.

#2
💪

Core Endurance (Sit-ups)

Importance: 0.141

Sit-up count emerged as the second-most predictive feature. A strong core correlates with higher class placement and better broad jump performance, underscoring the role of muscular endurance in athletic classification.

#3
⚖️

Body Composition (BMI & Fat %)

Importance: 0.119

Body fat percentage is the strongest negative predictor. Lower body fat, combined with a healthy BMI, is a consistent marker of superior physical performance across all class boundaries in the dataset.

#4
🤝

Grip Force

Importance: 0.081

Grip strength serves as a powerful proxy for overall muscular strength. Our analysis reveals a significant correlation between grip force and broad jump distance, making it a reliable indicator of explosive power readiness.
